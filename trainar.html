TrainAR

Project

As part of a collaboration between University of Sydney and various leading business partners, I had the opportunity to participate in a project offered by Swisslog that focused on Augmented Reality based training. This involved demonstrating the potential of the Microsoft Hololens, and providing a prototypic solution to help improve the training of new pickers at warehouse based facilities.

Team

Jung A (Monica) Lee, Isabelle Kohout, Gregory Wilcox, Lena Rehmann

Role

Scripting, UX-UI Design, Visual Design

Utilised Platforms

Unity, 3DSMax, C#

TrainAR is a training application that takes new trainees through a simulated pallet picking course. Each module consists of a different pallet to work with, teaching them of efficient stacking methods while the trainee also physically works alongside the application to help inculcate these techniques.

3D models and instructional audio are used to demonstrate efficient pallet stacking structures, taking the user through the pallet one product at a time. Voice commands and simple hand gestures are used to progress through the application.

Efficient placement for each individual product is visually instructed.

TrainAR places emphasis on visual education. By providing simple 3D models users can easily visualise the structure behind efficient pallet stacking, as well as experience the feeling of being taught personally with the audio clip educating them on relevant stacking rules. The user friendly interface and its' potential for further gamification was designed with the aim to improve overall picking speed, adaptability to work with Swisslog's currently implemented system, and enthusiasm in the workplace.
